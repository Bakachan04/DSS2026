[ 01 / VISION ]

Immersive hands-on design learning in the heart of Delhi.

The Design Summer School (DSS), organized by the Human-Centered Design (HCD) Association of IIIT Delhi, offers a series of interactive, expert-led workshops on design and related fields, including Human-Centered AI, Sensing, character sketching, photography, and XR/AR/VR design.

Curated for students, professionals, and design enthusiasts, this program provides a premium hands-on learning opportunity to build design solutions for real-world problems. Prepare for expert mentoring, high-impact tutorials, and networking with the brightest creative minds in Summer 2026.

[ 2025 // DAY 01 // KEYNOTE ]

Design Thinking & Ideation

The summer school launched with an inspiring opening ceremony and keynote address by Dr. Srinivasan Venkataraman from IIT Delhi. The talk established the foundations of design theory, creative cognition, and rapid ideation frameworks.

Key Highlights: Keynote address, cognitive design paradigms, ideation frameworks
[ 2025 // DAY 01 // HUMAN-CENTERED DESIGN ]

HCD & Interactive Research

Dr. Richa Gupta from IIT Delhi guided participants through the core principles of Human-Centered Design. Teams investigated real-world empathy mapping, interactive accessibility, and user-centric problem definitions.

Key Highlights: Empathy mapping, accessibility principles, interactive research methods
[ 2025 // DAY 01 // AI WORKSHOP ]

AI in Design Thinking

An interactive, hands-on workshop led by Mr. Arunesh Singh of Superlinked. Participants experimented with state-of-the-art generative AI tools to accelerate design ideation, synthesize user data, and build initial concept drafts.

Key Highlights: Generative AI workflows, data synthesis, rapid concept drafting
[ 2025 // DAY 02 // VISUAL SKETCHING ]

Character Sketching & Anatomy

Renowned Indian cartoonist Mr. Manoj Sinha introduced the fundamentals of character sketching. Attendees explored form drafting, facial proportions, and the basic skeletal structures that bring illustrated characters to life.

Key Highlights: Anatomy proportions, gesture drawing, pencil drafting techniques
[ 2025 // DAY 02 // CARTOONING ]

Visual Narratives & Cartooning

A deep dive session with Mr. Manoj Sinha on translating abstract concepts into cartoon strips. Participants learned how to sequence storyboards, apply humor, and use simple ink line-weight changes to convey complex expressions.

Key Highlights: Storyboard sequencing, comedic timing, visual rhythm and expression
[ 2025 // DAY 02 // CREATIVE METHODS ]

Design Methods of Creativity

Dr. Sonal Keshwani from IISc Bangalore conducted a structured workshop on systematic creative processes. Teams engaged in cognitive exercises to bypass creative blocks and applied engineering frameworks to brainstorm solutions.

Key Highlights: Cognitive mapping, structured brainstorming, creative confidence building
[ 2025 // DAY 03 // COGNITIVE HCI ]

Sensing & Cognitive HCI

Dr. Kalpana Shankhwar from National Taiwan University presented advanced research on human-computer interaction and cognitive design. The session focused on the translation of cognitive signals into intuitive interface designs.

Key Highlights: Cognitive signal feedback, user experience frameworks, physiological UX
[ 2025 // DAY 03 // BCI INTERFACES ]

Brain-Computer Interfaces

An immersive, live demonstration of Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI) led by Dr. Priyanka Jain and the Neuro-Cognitive AI & XR Group from C-DAC, MeitY. Participants conducted live EEG trials and experienced brainwave-driven feedback loops.

Key Highlights: Live EEG monitoring, brainwave interaction trials, C-DAC neuro-AI showcase
[ 2025 // DAY 03 // INTERACTIVE ARTS ]

New Media Design & Arts

Dr. Gowdham Prabhakar from IIT Kanpur led a technical workshop on New Media Design using TouchDesigner. Attendees built projection mapping setups and designed interactive, audio-reactive digital installations.

Key Highlights: TouchDesigner pipelines, projection mapping calibration, audio-reactive design
[ 2025 // DAY 04 // PHOTOGRAPHY ]

Storytelling & Lens Curation

Senior NGMA photographer Mr. Bijendra Kumar and Mr. Anoop Ratn guided participants on depth of field, visual framing, and camera mechanics. The session explored how camera settings shape narrative perspectives.

Key Highlights: Depth of field studies, manual camera settings, rules of composition
[ 2025 // DAY 04 // LENS NARRATIVES ]

Light Curation & Composition

Part two of the photography masterclass focused on studio lighting and composition. Mr. Bijendra Kumar and Mr. Anoop Ratn helped participants build complex visual stories and conducted critique sessions of peer photo submissions.

Key Highlights: Studio lighting setups, photographic narratives, peer review portfolios
[ 2025 // DAY 04 // MOBILE CONTENT ]

Mobile Photography & Videography

An active outdoor workshop led by Mr. Aman Samuel focusing on capturing professional-quality visual assets using mobile cameras. Attendees learned on-the-go framing, sound capture, and post-production techniques.

Key Highlights: Mobile camera controls, audio recording techniques, mobile editing workflows
[ 2025 // DAY 05 // MULTISENSORY ]

Multisensory Interface Technologies

Dr. Madhan Kumar Vasudevan from IIT Roorkee introduced advanced multisensory interfaces. The session examined haptic feedback, affective touch, and design evaluation frameworks to build products that appeal to multiple senses.

Key Highlights: Haptic feedback setups, affective touch design, multisensory evaluation
[ 2025 // DAY 05 // VR INTERACTION ]

VR Systems & Interaction

Dr. Anmol Srivastava from IIT Guwahati led a technical session on virtual reality. Participants explored immersive spaces, calibrated head-mounted displays, and designed interactive haptic feedback loops in virtual scenes.

Key Highlights: Unity VR configuration, spatial interaction mapping, haptic device calibration

Scientist F (Associate Director) & Head of the Neuro-Cognitive AI and XR Group, C-DAC, MeitY, Government of India.

We are honored to host Dr. Priyanka Jain, Scientist "F" (Associate Director) and Head of the Neuro-Cognitive AI & XR Group at C-DAC, MeitY, Government of India, for a special session titled "Vivan: A Vibrant Brain." With 24 years of AI research experience, recipient of the AWSAR-2019 Award, and leader of cutting-edge, government-funded projects in brain, computer interfaces and behavioral analysis, Dr. Jain has delivered over 25 invited talks, authored 73 research publications, secured 14 copyrights, and contributed to bilingual scientific literature. Frequently asked

The Design Summer School is open to everyone whether you're a beginner or already have some design experience. No prior background is required to attend.
The registration deadline for DSS'25 is 30th June 2025.
To receive a certificate, you must attend at least 75% of the sessions. This ensures that you gain the maximum benefit from the program and can apply what you learn effectively.
Registration is required for the entire program, and there is no scope for partial registrations for individual sessions. However, once registered, you are free to attend sessions based on your interest and availability.
The Design Summer School is a 5-days event where the attendees get to participate in interesting talks, lectures, and workshops on various aspects of design. DSS is curated for students, industry professionals, designers, artists, and anyone interested in exploring design and its allied fields.
